## Transcript

Three years ago I was working seventy-hour weeks and I was proud of it. I would tell people how busy I was like it was an achievement. I wore exhaustion like a badge.

Then one morning I could not get out of bed. Not because I was sick. I just could not make myself move. My body had decided it was done. I lay there for two hours staring at the ceiling. I missed three meetings. I did not care.

I had been building a business for four years. A real one. Revenue, clients, a small team. And I had optimised it entirely around my own destruction. Every system I built required me to be present. Every decision ran through me. I had created a machine that could only run on my suffering.

My doctor told me I was clinically burned out. I told her I did not have time for that. She looked at me for a long moment and said: you do not have a time problem. You have a belief problem. You believe your value comes from how much you sacrifice.

That sentence broke something open in me.

I spent the next year rebuilding everything. Not the business — myself. I hired people to make decisions without me. I set a hard stop at six in the evening. I took Saturdays completely offline. My revenue dropped twenty percent in the first quarter. And then it grew forty percent in the next three.

I do not work less because I am lazy. I work less because I finally understood that the machine only runs well when the person inside it is okay.
