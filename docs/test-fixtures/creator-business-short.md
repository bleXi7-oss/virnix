## Transcript

The biggest mistake I made in my first two years as a creator was treating my audience like a number.

I obsessed over follower count. I checked it every morning. I made decisions based on it. I turned down brand deals because the money seemed too low for my size. I said no to collaborations because the other creator had fewer followers than me.

Here is what I missed: audience size means nothing without trust.

I figured this out the hard way. I had 80,000 followers and launched my first digital product. I expected to make at least thirty or forty thousand dollars. I made eleven hundred. I thought the product was wrong. I thought the pricing was wrong. I thought the launch timing was wrong.

It was none of those things. The problem was that my audience did not trust me yet. I had built a following around entertainment, not around expertise. People liked my content. They did not believe I could solve their problems.

I spent the next six months creating nothing but practical, specific, useful content. No entertainment. No trends. No hooks designed to go viral. Just honest, useful explanations of what I actually knew. My growth slowed down. My engagement looked worse on paper. But something different was happening.

When I launched my second product, I had 74,000 followers. Fewer than before. I made sixty-two thousand dollars in four days.

The only thing that changed was trust. Stop growing your audience. Start earning their trust. The revenue follows automatically.
